Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about laser marking for thermal insulation strips

🔍 General Questions

Why do thermal insulation strips need permanent marking?

Permanent marking is required for traceability, quality control, and compliance with international standards like EN 14024 (Europe) and ASTM E2181 (North America). Markings typically include batch numbers, production dates, manufacturer identification, and technical specifications. This ensures that every strip can be traced back to its production source, which is critical for warranty claims, quality audits, and building certification.

What material are thermal break strips made of, and why is laser marking suitable?

Standard thermal break strips are made of PA66 GF25 (Polyamide 66 reinforced with 25% glass fiber). Fiber laser marking is ideal for this material because it creates a permanent, high-contrast mark through molecular-level interaction without weakening the strip's structural integrity. Unlike ink printing or embossing, laser marking doesn't introduce chemical contaminants or physical stress points.

Is laser marking better than ink printing or embossing?

Yes, for several reasons:

  • Permanence: Laser marks are UV-resistant, chemical-resistant, and won't fade or smudge
  • Precision: Achieves 0.01mm accuracy, allowing detailed codes and logos
  • Zero Consumables: No ink, solvents, or ribbons required
  • Non-Contact: No physical stress on the PA66 strip structure
  • Eco-Friendly: No chemical waste or disposal requirements

⚙️ Technical Questions

What's the difference between single-side and double-side marking?

Single-side marking lasers one face of the thermal strip, which is sufficient for most standard applications. Double-side marking simultaneously marks both faces, which is required when strips will be installed in configurations where both sides are visible, or when dual-side traceability is mandated by local building codes. Double-side systems typically increase production efficiency by 30-40%.

What does "single traction" vs "double traction" mean?

This refers to the feeding mechanism:

  • Single Traction: One motor drives the strip through the marking zone. Suitable for standard lengths and stable production lines.
  • Double Traction: Two synchronized motors at entry and exit points ensure perfect tension control. Essential for longer strips, thinner profiles, or high-speed production where strip deformation must be prevented.
What laser source do you use, and what's its lifespan?

We use IPG fiber laser sources (industry standard for reliability and beam quality). The typical lifespan is 100,000+ hours of operation. Laser power options range from 20W to 50W depending on marking speed and depth requirements. The laser module is maintenance-free and doesn't require alignment or gas refills.

Can the machine mark QR codes, barcodes, and logos?

Yes. Our laser marking systems support vector graphics, raster images, QR codes, DataMatrix, Code 128, and custom logos. The control software allows easy import of design files and automatic batch number/date generation. Marking content can be updated instantly via USB or network connection without mechanical adjustments.
